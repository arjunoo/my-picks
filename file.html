My favourite Film a clef's. (which means French for "film with a key" is a film describing real life, behind a façade of fiction."Key" in this context means a table one can use to swap out the names.) Active names in this genre include Mani Ratnam with his psuedo biopics and Gautham Vasudev Menon for Tamil and the legendary Orson Welles whose advice:"don't watch too many films" is something I must have really listened to..

Scarface, Adaptation and Annie Hall are too films in the same genre and hold good as reccos..

Prabhudeva: the man and the myth ranked.. (some of these follow the baasha template effortlessly)

Confession: Rowdy Rathore is forever my favourite pulp action film,I have watched it everyday from 2012-2017.. Wanted too is a classic tough guy soft heart film,special shoutout to Prakash Raj who I admire too much, R Rajkumar only for two songs, rest of it is passable. It is my dream to be a part of a Prabhudeva choreographed song, whatever it takes..

quality Bollywood indie, exceptions to the rule of three is warranted here

this is a starter pack of sorts.. watch out for Nawazzudin and onions in The Lunchbox, Randeep Hooda and of course Alia Bhatt who was barely 21 when she held her own in Highway, a fantastic Imitiaz Ali film and of course Rajkummar Rao and Bhumi Pednekar who are two indie machines, Qala is beautifully staged and written, Nil Battey Sannata has a winning sensitivity, Dear Zindagi is extraordinary in its use of Alia Bhatt and Shah Rukh Khan.. Manorama Six Feet Under is a desert noir film that's enjoyable as is Masoom and Ek Chalis Ki Last Local, the whole of Anurag Kashyap's filmography and Batra's Photograph fit this bill too.

hilarious Bollywood indie

this is Bollywood letting its otherwise glammed up hair down to give us laugh riots.. I'm biased towards Ghanchakkar and Pattakha since both are made by serious filmmakers being resonably hilarious, but Delhi Belly is wicked humor at its best.

favourite Malayalam films

let me clarify some stuff: I've watched Aavesham and Romancham, both are crowd induced noisy theatre films but worthy comedies, Mahesinte Prathikaaram has been reccomended to me multiple times so it is great too, Malik is Nayakan in Malayalam but that opening shot is bliss, Ayyappanum Koshiyum is a Western only as one of the characters in the 3hr film states, but I need no more reasons to love Prithivraj Sukumaran, the fact that he's ridiclously great at everything is enough, oh and I watched Manujummel Boys, I know people who don't like it but technically it is a resounding achievement, I've watched Premam too but that was again a cliched choice so no.. moving on

great Kannada indie films(there are six of these, forgive me for this is an obligation I owe my state)

Rangitaranga is peak horror,Ulidavaru Kandante is marvellous but requires prior knowledge like Rashomon, Om and Tarantino's selected filmmography, Hemanth M Rao has made some top notch films but started out with Godhi Banna.. also watch Kavaludaari, the directorials of Rishab Shetty can be binged, Raj B Shetty is getting there no doubt, if you watch Operation Alamelamma also watch that director's first film, Lucia is a riff on Requiem For A Dream and Memento but has so much local flavour that its amazing, Aachar and Co is a love letter to Bangalore, I like to call it a Wenkatmurthy Anderson film, watch and you'll know why.. this can in a sense be called the main indie films, but there are so many emerging voices, the list would be far longer..

progressive Kannada films you might have never heard of

Shankar Nag is a revered filmmaker here, any film he has made is splendid, he also directed Malgudi Days with Girish Karnad (another legend) who very early on made the Kurosawaesque film that was supremely captivating with Shankar Nag ironically despite year of release and Upendra is Upendra, you can't understand him but only watch him work his magic.. (If however you look deeper, you'll find names such as Girish Kasarvalli, Puttanna Kanagal etc. whose films are classics too.)
